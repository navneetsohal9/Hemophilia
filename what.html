What Is The Difference?

Difference In Blood Clotting

The picture is showing the difference between normal blood clotting vs hemophilia blood clotting. The difference is the fibrin. In normal blood clotting, the fibrin is sealed over the wound and prevents the bleeding from happeneing any further. In hemophilia blood clotting, the fibrin is not stable so, the bleeding continues.



How Does One Get It?

Genetics

"Hemophilia affects your X gene. In a male, they have one X gene and one Y gene. A female has two X genes. In females, the second X acts normal, therefore the female becomes a carrier. In a male, they only have one X so when it is affected, he will have hemophilia.



A male passes When he has a daughter, he will pass down the affected X gene and all of his daughters will be carriers. When a female that is a carrier has a son, he has a 50/50 chance of whether or not he will get the affected X.


When she has a daughter she has a 50/50 chance of passing down the affected X gene. If a male with Hemophilia has children with a woman who is a carrier, their sons have a 50/50 shot of getting the affected X gene from the mother. When they have daughters, they will get the affected X gene from the father and have a 50/50 shot of getting the affected X gene from the mother. The daughters will be carriers, but if they get the affected X gene from the mother then both X genes will be affected and they will have Hemophilia." (https://www.my46.org/intro/x-linked-inheritance)
