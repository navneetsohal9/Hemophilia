Who Started All Of This?

QUEEN VICTORIA!



Year Image (HOVER OVER IMAGE) What Happened?
1800's Queen Victoria passed on the mutation to royal families of Spain, Germany and Russia. It is called the Royal Disease because hemophilia first spread through families of royal descent.
1950's The first medical treatment was using fresh frozen plasma. Patients recieved plasma infusions which contained traces of coagulation factors. Patients needed more lenghty infusions which resulted in extreme stress on the cardiovascular system, and usually resulted in heart attacks.
1965 Judith Pool helped the problem with frozen plasma infusions by discovering cryoprecipitate. This discovery reduced the volume of the infusion and allowed outpatient treatment for the first time.
1970's Prophylactic home therapy became possible. Coagulants could be seperated from pool plasma which allowed easy storage and accurate dosages of coagulant factors.
1973 The Hemophilia Care Act of 1973's goal was to be federally funded for hemophilia treatment centers with access to surgeons, orthopedists and physical therapists.
1980's Pooling plasma from several donors infected hemophilia patients with blood borne disease such as HIV and Hepatitis.
1992 Recombinant DNA technology eliminates the use of human plasma. The Prophylactic home therapy becomes safe again.
2000's As for the last decade, hemophilia treatments are still being modified. Recombinant therapy aims to eliminate inhibitor antibodies. Gene therapy promises a cure to genetically alter the body so that it can produce the missing coagulant factors.
